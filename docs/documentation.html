About earthLings

The earthLings project is building a global and multi-lingual computational linguistic atlas. A linguistic atlas involves mapping both language (who speaks what where) and dialects (who uses what variants where). A computational linguistic atlas does this using automated and reproducible analysis of very large digital corpora (~420 billion words from the web and ~14 billion words from social media). While traditional approaches to language and dialect mapping require asking people about their language behaviours, a computational approach involves observing their language behaviours on a large scale. This is a global atlas because it covers most of the countries in the world and 10k cities. This is a multi-lingual atlas because it covers 464 different languages.
